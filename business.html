A practical guide

Copilot, Cowork, Studio & Scout: what to use when

Most tasks work in more than one tool. This is the simplest way to pick the right one and graduate as the work gets bigger. Every claim is grounded in Microsoft Learn or microsoft.com, with links at the bottom. Preview features are labelled and may change.

01 · The four tools

At a glance

Pick by how the work shows up: a question, a process, a repeated team task, or something that needs to run on its own.

💬
Microsoft 365 Copilot
Your everyday AI assistant in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams.
  • You ask, it responds
  • Grounded in your work content and the web
  • Includes ready agents like Researcher and Analyst
🤝
Copilot Cowork
An AI coworker that actually does the work, end-to-end, from one prompt.
  • Sends emails, books meetings, creates files, posts in Teams
  • You approve each action before it happens
  • Loads built-in and custom skills, and can use approved plugins
🛠️
Copilot Studio
Low-code platform to build and govern your own agents.
  • For repeated processes a whole team or department uses
  • Connect to CRM, ERP, and other line-of-business systems
  • Publish to Teams, web, or your own channels
🛰️
Microsoft Scout
An always-on agent that stays ahead of your day, acting on your behalf.
  • Proactive: works without being prompted each time
  • Spots risks early, blocks prep time, coordinates across time zones
  • Runs under its own governed identity with Purview controls

04 · Get better results from Cowork

How to ask Cowork well

A vague prompt forces Cowork to guess. Five small parts make the difference.

1. Outcome
One sentence describing what done looks like.
2. Inputs
The people, files, sites, or time ranges to use.
3. Definition of done
The concrete deliverable, sent, saved, or scheduled.
4. Constraints
Things to avoid or honour (template, length, audience).
5. Approval scope
Which actions you want to review explicitly.
Before

"Help me prep for my offsite next week."

After

Outcome: A briefing pack and a draft agenda for the offsite on June 12. Inputs: The offsite invite, the three pre-reads attached to it, and emails from the offsite distribution list in the last two weeks. Definition of done: A one-page Word doc saved to my OneDrive Offsite folder, and a draft agenda emailed to my direct reports for feedback. Constraints: No external attendees. Approval scope: Default, review the email before sending.

06 · Before you build

Building your own agent should be the last step, not the first.

Simplest

Can a saved prompt do it?

If a repeatable prompt handles the job, save it and reuse it. Microsoft 365 Copilot also lets you schedule a saved prompt to run on a cadence you set (requires a Microsoft 365 Copilot licence).

2
Reuse

Is there a ready Microsoft agent?

Researcher and Analyst are already there for Microsoft 365 Copilot licence holders. Check the agent store first.

3
Scale on demand

Does Cowork already cover it?

For end-to-end processes that produce several deliverables, Cowork often handles it without a custom build.

4
Build it

Agent Builder or Copilot Studio

Agent Builder for yourself or a small team. Studio for a department, organisation, or external customers.

Cowork tasks in action

Cowork tasks in action

What light, medium, and heavy looks like with Copilot Cowork.

Light means quick everyday handoffs Cowork can finish in minutes, pulling from a source or two. Medium means recurring multi-step work that fills your week, reasoning across several sources. Heavy means the big multi-part work you would block out serious time for, producing a full set of artifacts.

These displayed examples use Microsoft 365 data and files. Plugin-dependent tasks are intentionally excluded here, although Cowork can be extended through Microsoft 365 App Store plugins such as Fabric IQ, Dynamics 365 Sales, monday.com, Miro, Prezi, and Templafy where enabled by your admin.

Light: Quick handoffs, a small number of sources, and minutes to finish.
Medium: Recurring multi-step work across several sources.
Heavy: Big jobs with multiple artifacts and approvals.

Light Rebalance your week and protect focus time

Why light: Quick, single-purpose handoff. Cowork pulls from one or two sources and returns a ready-to-use artifact in minutes.

Goal: Take control of a fragmented calendar before it takes control of your week.

Outcome: A rebalanced schedule that protects focus time, prioritizes high-impact meetings, and resolves conflicts, without manually negotiating your own availability.

Prompt:

Help me organize my week. Review my Outlook calendar.
First, show me a summary: Total meetings and hours spent, where I have focus time (2+ hours), and days with the most meetings.
Before taking changes, ask clarifying questions: my manager and management chain, note if they are on a meeting, how many attendees, what I'm trying to accomplish this week, how to prioritize conflicts, personal commitments, and which meetings to decline or shorten.
Then show proposed changes with explanations: meetings to accept/decline/reschedule, conflicts to resolve (including emailing organizers), and focus blocks to add. Start with highest-impact changes first.
Once I approve each change, make edits directly in my calendar one at a time.
Follow-up prompts: Accept and decline meetings. Create a customer meeting prep document.

Execution flow:

  1. Calendar -> Summarize meeting load + focus time
  2. Clarify -> Weekly goals + constraints
  3. Evaluate -> Meeting impact + conflicts
  4. Propose -> Accept / decline / reschedule
  5. Recommend -> Add focus blocks
  6. Approve -> Proposed changes
  7. Execute -> Apply updates one-by-one

Note:

  • To automate this, save the prompt, then in Microsoft 365 Copilot hover the saved prompt and choose "Schedule this prompt". Use a skill separately when you want the same workflow instructions reused.
